Sandwich

A sandwich is a dish that typically consists of meat, cheese, or vegetables placed between or on top of two slices of bread.

The term "sandwich" was adopted in the 18th century after John Montagu, the 4th Earl of Sandwich, is said to have invented it. According to Mary Isin, author of Bountiful Empire: A History of Ottoman Cuisine, the idea of rolling bread with a filling may have originated in Turkish culture, and the Earl may have gotten the idea to ask a restaurant server to make him a sandwich after traveling to Turkey in the mid-17th century
